ToS;DR

("Terms-of-Service; Didn't Read" - under construction)

A crowd-reading hub for Terms-of-Service of websites.

This website is a hub where people can share their opinion on the terms of service of websites. We discuss a changing list of topics. The points indicate how important each item is. You can vote them up or down if you feel some topic is over- or under-represented. You can also propose new topics on the new topics discussion page. Currently there are 11:
  • BY: Attribution. Like the 'BY' in CC licenses
    • We own data you submit (0 points)
    • You own your own data (90 points)

  • PRIV: PRIVacy. Whether you keep sole control over the data you own
    • You have to license your data to us (0 points)
    • We will not get a license beyond what is necessary for running the service (90 points)

  • DEL: About data retention vs. DELetion
    • We might keep a copy of your data, even if you don\'t want us to (0 points)
    • We will delete both your primary data and your secondary data quickly after you close your account (90 points)

  • END: What happens when we END your service
    • We do not provide an appeal procedure in case we end our service to you (0 points)
    • We may suspend your account at any time, but will allow you to appeal before an independent court or panel (50 points)
    • We will not end or suspend your account, unless required to do so by law (90 points)

  • CEN: What happens when we CENsor your data
    • We do not provide an appeal procedure in case we censor your data on our service (0 points)
    • We may censor your primary data at any time, but will allow you to appeal before an independent court or panel (50 points)
    • We will not censor your primary data except when required to do so by law (90 points)

  • GOV: What happens when the GOVernment (law enforcement) wants to see your data
    • (0 points)
    • We will try to help you get a chance to defend yourself when law enforcement requests access to your data (90 points)

  • OPP: You down with Other People's Purposes? Or should we stick to using your data to serve only your purposes?
    • (0 points)
    • We may use aggregated data for the purposes of people other than yourself, but then you will not be identifiable (50 points)
    • We will not use your primary nor your secondary data for serving any purposes other than yours (90 points)

  • DL: Data Liberation
    • You can export your primary data at any time, but to a format that even we ourselves don\'t support for import (0 points)
    • You can import and export your primary data at any time (90 points)

  • EQ: EQuality
    • Whether this service is available to you may depend on your location, race, gender, nationality, residence, political views, religion, sexual orientation, or criminal record (0 points)
    • We are actively and demonstrably working to make this service available to more human beings, regardless of their location, race, gender, nationality, residence, political views, religion, sexual orientation, and criminal record (50 points)
    • We are colour blind to your location, race, gender, nationality, residence, political views, religion, sexual orientation, and criminal record (90 points)

  • ANON: Using disposable pseudonyms for each session (ANONymity), or one multi-session pseudonym (nickname)
    • You have to give us your real identity, uniquely linking your account to you as a physical person (0 points)
    • We allow you to use nicknames towards other users, as long as you do tell us your real identity (50 points)
    • We allow you to use a pseudonym, whether used during multiple sessions, or only once (90 points)

  • DIST: Whether you can install your own node of this service, and interact with people on other DISTributed nodes like the current one
    • This is the only instance of this service. Either the source code is secret, or you\'re not allowed to run your own instance of it (0 points)
    • The software behind this service is FOSS. Be aware though, that each node forms a restrictive island of user interaction (50 points)
    • This service is distributed; using a a different instance will not give you significant loss of interaction with other users (90 points)

The table

For each of the topics mentioned above, and for each major website, there is a wiki page on which you can discuss your interpretation with others. Whenever there is a concensus, this is displayed in the table. It's also available in cross-origin JSON, for use in web apps and plugins.

Classification

Whenever there is concensus on all points about a website, a classification between "ToS;DR|A" (good) and "ToS;DR|G" (bad) is issued. This is based on the points mentioned above, such that the best known website (currently: ... with ... points) is the start of class A, and the worst known website (currently ... with ... points) is the end of class G.
